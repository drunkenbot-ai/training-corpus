India became a free country on the fifteenth of August in the year nineteen hundred and forty seven. It was a day of great joy and great sorrow. Joy because the British rule had ended after nearly two hundred years. Sorrow because the country was divided into two parts. India and Pakistan were born as separate nations. Millions of people had to move from one side to the other. Many lost their homes. Many lost their lives. The pain of partition was deep and it did not heal quickly.

The first Prime Minister of India was Jawaharlal Nehru. He was a man of great learning and vision. He had spent many years in prison for the freedom struggle. He was a follower of Mahatma Gandhi. Gandhi ji was the father of the nation. He believed in non violence and truth. But Gandhi ji was not happy with the partition. He had wanted a united India. Yet he accepted the decision of the Congress party leaders. He stayed in India to heal the wounds of the people.

On the night of independence, Nehru gave a famous speech. He said that India would awake to life and freedom. He spoke about the tryst with destiny. He said that the time had come to redeem the pledge of the nation. The people gathered in large numbers to hear him. They waved the tricolour flag. They sang the national anthem. They felt proud to be Indians.

But the first few months were very difficult. The country had to deal with refugees. People came from Pakistan in large numbers. They had no food, no shelter, and no money. The government had to set up camps for them. Many families were separated. Many children became orphans. The leaders worked day and night to help the people.

The second great leader of that time was Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. He was the Deputy Prime Minister and the Home Minister. He was called the Iron Man of India. He took on the task of uniting the princely states. At that time, there were more than five hundred princely states. These were small kingdoms ruled by Maharajas and Nawabs. They had the option to join India or Pakistan or remain independent. Sardar Patel used his skill and strength to bring them into India. He convinced most of them to merge. Some like Hyderabad and Junagadh were difficult. He had to use force in some cases. But in the end, he succeeded. India became one united country.

The state of Kashmir was a special case. It was ruled by Maharaja Hari Singh. He did not decide immediately. Pakistan sent tribesmen to invade Kashmir. The Maharaja then asked for help from India. He signed the instrument of accession. India sent its army to protect Kashmir. The war with Pakistan began. The United Nations was asked to intervene. A ceasefire was declared. But the issue of Kashmir remained unresolved. It became a long standing dispute.

The constitution of India was a very important document. It was drafted by a Constituent Assembly. The chairman of the drafting committee was Dr. B.R. Ambedkar. He was a great scholar and a leader of the Dalits. He worked very hard to give India a constitution that would be just and fair. The constitution was adopted on the twenty sixth of January in the year nineteen hundred and fifty. India became a republic on that day. It is celebrated as Republic Day.

The constitution gave every citizen the right to vote. It promised equality and freedom. It abolished untouchability. It gave special rights to the backward classes. It made India a secular country. This meant that the state would not favour any religion. All religions would be treated equally. It was a bold and progressive step.

The first general election was held in nineteen hundred and fifty two. It was a huge task. India had a large population. Many people could not read or write. The Election Commission used symbols for parties. A symbol like a hand or a lotus or a pair of bullocks was used. People could identify the symbol and vote. The Congress party won a big majority. Nehru became the Prime Minister again.

Nehru had a vision for India. He wanted India to become a modern and industrialised country. He believed in science and technology. He set up many institutes of higher learning. The Indian Institutes of Technology were established. The Indian Institutes of Management came later. He also started the planning process. The Five Year Plans were introduced. These plans focused on agriculture, industry, and infrastructure. Dams and power plants were built. The Bhakra Nangal dam was one of the biggest projects. It was called a temple of modern India.

But the early years were not easy. India had to face many challenges. There was a shortage of food grains. The country had to import wheat from the United States. The government had to ration food. People stood in long queues for bread and rice. The economy was weak. The industry was not developed. Many people lived in poverty.

The social problems were also great. Caste discrimination was still present. Women had few rights. Child marriage was common. Illiteracy was high. The government passed many laws to change these things. The Hindu Marriage Act was passed. The Hindu Succession Act gave daughters the right to inherit property. The government also promoted family planning. It was a slow process but change was coming.

Nehru died in nineteen hundred and sixty four. His death was a great blow to the nation. Millions of people wept. He had led India for seventeen years. He was a charismatic leader. He was loved by the people. After his death, Lal Bahadur Shastri became the Prime Minister. He was a small man but he had a big heart. He was known for his honesty and simplicity. He gave the slogan Jai Jawan Jai Kisan. It meant hail the soldier and hail the farmer. He encouraged the country to be strong in defence and self reliant in food.

In nineteen hundred and sixty five, India had a war with Pakistan. The war was fought over Kashmir. The fighting was intense. The Indian army fought bravely. The ceasefire was declared after some weeks. Shastri went to Tashkent to sign a peace agreement. But he died soon after in Tashkent. His death was sudden and mysterious. Many people were shocked.

Indira Gandhi became the Prime Minister after Shastri. She was the daughter of Nehru. She had been a minister in his cabinet. She was a strong and determined woman. She became one of the most powerful leaders in the world. She had to deal with many problems. The economy was still struggling. The Congress party had internal divisions. The opposition was growing stronger.

In nineteen hundred and sixty seven, there were major elections. The Congress party won but with a reduced majority. Indira Gandhi had to face many challenges. There was a split in the Congress party. The old guard opposed her. She formed a new party called Congress R. She got the support of the people. She promised to fight poverty and inequality. She gave the slogan Garibi Hatao. It meant remove poverty. This made her very popular.

In nineteen hundred and seventy one, there was another war with Pakistan. This time it was over East Pakistan. The people of East Pakistan wanted freedom. They were being oppressed by the Pakistani army. Many refugees came to India. India decided to help them. The Indian army fought a short war. They defeated the Pakistani army. East Pakistan became the new country of Bangladesh. It was a great victory for India. Indira Gandhi became a hero.

After the war, India conducted its first nuclear test in nineteen hundred and seventy four. It was called Smiling Buddha. India showed that it had nuclear capability. The world was surprised. Some countries criticised India. Others were quiet. India said it was for peaceful purposes. But the test changed the balance of power in the region.

In nineteen hundred and seventy five, a big crisis came. The opposition parties accused Indira Gandhi of election fraud. A court in Allahabad found her guilty. She was asked to step down. But she did not step down. She declared a state of emergency. She suspended the constitution. She arrested her political opponents. The press was censored. Many civil rights were taken away. It was a dark period for democracy.

The emergency lasted for twenty one months. Many people were tortured in jail. The government became very unpopular. In nineteen hundred and seventy seven, Indira Gandhi lifted the emergency. She called for fresh elections. The opposition united under a new party called Janata Party. They fought against her. The people voted them to power. Indira Gandhi and her party lost the election. It was the first time the Congress party was out of power.

Morarji Desai became the Prime Minister. He was the first non Congress Prime Minister. He was a follower of Gandhian principles. He tried to restore democracy. He also tried to improve relations with Pakistan and China. But his government was unstable. Different leaders fought among themselves. The government fell in less than three years.

Charan Singh became the Prime Minister for a short time. He was a farmer leader. But he could not hold the coalition together. He resigned after a few months. Indira Gandhi came back to power in nineteen hundred and eighty. She won a big majority. The people had forgiven her.

In nineteen hundred and eighty four, there was a very sad event. Indira Gandhi sent the army to the Golden Temple in Amritsar. Some militants were hiding there. The operation was called Blue Star. Many people died. It hurt the sentiments of the Sikhs. On the thirty first of October, Indira Gandhi was shot by her own Sikh bodyguards. She died. It was a terrible moment. The country was in shock.

Rajiv Gandhi became the Prime Minister. He was the son of Indira Gandhi. He was young and modern. He wanted to bring computers and technology to India. He talked about the future. He tried to improve relations with other countries. He also worked on the Panchayati Raj system. This gave more power to local governments.

But Rajiv Gandhi also faced problems. The Bofors scandal broke out. It was a defence deal involving bribes. His reputation was damaged. In nineteen hundred and eighty nine, his party lost the election. The next government was led by V.P. Singh. He was from the Janata Dal. He tried to help the backward classes. He introduced reservations for Other Backward Classes. This was based on the Mandal Commission report. It caused a lot of anger among the forward castes. Many students protested.

In nineteen hundred and ninety, there was a crisis in Kashmir. Militancy began to grow. Many young men took up arms. The government used the army to control the situation. It was a long and bloody conflict. Many innocent people died. The issue remained unresolved.

Chandra Shekhar became the Prime Minister for a short while. Then there were more elections. The Congress party came back to power. Narasimha Rao became the Prime Minister in nineteen hundred and ninety one. He was a quiet and scholarly man. He was not very well known. But he did something remarkable. He introduced economic reforms. India had a balance of payment crisis. The country had very little foreign exchange. India had to pledge its gold to get a loan. Rao and his finance minister Manmohan Singh opened up the economy. They removed many licences and restrictions. They allowed foreign investment. They privatised some state owned companies. This changed India forever.

The economy started to grow faster. More jobs were created. The middle class expanded. People started buying cars, phones, and televisions. The old socialist model was replaced by a market model. It was a big change. Many people welcomed it. Some people opposed it. But the reforms continued.

In nineteen hundred and ninety two, there was a very tragic event. The Babri Masjid in Ayodhya was demolished. It was an old mosque. Many Hindu activists believed that it was built on the birthplace of Lord Ram. They tore down the mosque. This led to riots across the country. Muslims were attacked in many places. Thousands of people died. It was a shameful moment. The government could not prevent it. The Supreme Court later set up a commission to investigate.

In nineteen hundred and ninety three, there were bomb blasts in Mumbai. Many people were killed. It was linked to the underworld and Pakistan. The police investigated and caught many people. But the tensions between communities remained.

In nineteen hundred and ninety six, the Congress party lost again. The Bharatiya Janata Party became the largest party. But they did not have a majority. The government was formed by a United Front. H.D. Deve Gowda became the Prime Minister. He was from Karnataka. He was a farmer leader. His government did not last long. I.K. Gujral became the Prime Minister. He was a diplomat. He followed the Gujral doctrine. This meant good relations with neighbours. But his government also fell.

In nineteen hundred and ninety eight, the BJP formed a coalition government. Atal Bihari Vajpayee became the Prime Minister. He was a poet and a statesman. He was very popular. He formed the National Democratic Alliance. He completed five years in office. He did many good things. He built highways and improved telecommunications. He also conducted nuclear tests in nineteen hundred and ninety eight. India tested five nuclear bombs. It became a nuclear power. The world imposed sanctions. But India stood firm.

In nineteen hundred and ninety nine, there was a war in Kargil. Pakistani soldiers and militants crossed the Line of Control. They occupied high peaks. The Indian army fought back. It was a difficult war fought at high altitudes. The army drove them out. India won the war. Vajpayee’s popularity increased.

In two thousand and two, there was a terrible incident in Gujarat. A train carrying Hindu pilgrims was set on fire in Godhra. Many people died. In retaliation, there were riots across Gujarat. Thousands of Muslims were killed. The government was led by Narendra Modi as Chief Minister. He was accused of not doing enough to stop the violence. But he denied it. The Supreme Court later investigated and gave him a clean chit. But the issue remains controversial.

In two thousand and four, the elections were held. The BJP lost. The Congress party formed the United Progressive Alliance. Manmohan Singh became the Prime Minister. He was a well known economist. He had initiated the reforms of nineteen ninety one. He was a soft spoken and honest man. He served for ten years. His government did many things. They passed the Right to Information Act. They started the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act. They gave food security to the poor. They also signed a nuclear deal with the United States. India got access to nuclear technology. It was a landmark agreement.

But his government also faced corruption scandals. The Commonwealth Games scam, the 2G spectrum scam, and the coal block scam were big issues. The opposition attacked him. The media also criticised him. His popularity went down.

In two thousand and fourteen, the BJP won a landslide victory. Narendra Modi became the Prime Minister. He was the former Chief Minister of Gujarat. He promised development and good governance. He said he would bring investment and jobs. He gave the slogan Sabka Saath Sabka Vikas. It meant together with all, development for all. He started many schemes. He launched the Make in India program. He introduced GST to simplify taxes. He built toilets and houses for the poor. He started the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan for cleanliness.

In two thousand and sixteen, he made a big decision. He announced demonetisation. He banned the five hundred and one thousand rupee notes. It was a shock to everyone. People had to queue for hours to exchange notes. The economy was disrupted. Many people suffered. But he said it was to fight black money and corruption. The opposition criticised it. Some people supported it.

In two thousand and seventeen, the Goods and Services Tax was introduced. It was a major tax reform. It unified the tax system across the country. It made business easier. But there were initial problems. Small businesses found it hard to comply. Over time, the system improved.

In two thousand and nineteen, India carried out an air strike in Pakistan. A group called Jaish e Mohammad had attacked a convoy in Pulwama. Many soldiers died. The Indian Air Force struck a terrorist camp in Balakot. There was a dogfight between Indian and Pakistani planes. An Indian pilot was captured but later released. It was a tense situation.

In the same year, the government revoked the special status of Jammu and Kashmir. Article 370 was removed. Kashmir was divided into two union territories. It was a big change. The government said it would bring development. But many people in Kashmir were unhappy. There was a security lockdown. Internet was cut. The situation was controlled. But there were protests.

In two thousand and twenty, the world faced a pandemic. It was caused by the coronavirus. India went into a strict lockdown. Millions of people lost their jobs. Migrant workers had to walk hundreds of kilometres to reach their homes. The government gave free food and money to the poor. The healthcare system was stretched. Many people died. But India also produced its own vaccines. The vaccination drive was huge.

The years after independence have been full of change. India has grown a lot. The population is now over one billion. The economy is the fifth largest in the world. The middle class is big. The cities are modern. The villages have electricity and roads. Mobile phones and internet are common. Young people are educated and ambitious. They dream of a better future.

But India still has many problems. Poverty is still there. Millions of people do not get two meals a day. The gap between rich and poor is very wide. Caste discrimination is still a reality. Women are not safe in many places. The environment is being damaged. The air and water are polluted. The climate is changing. There are floods and droughts. Farmers are in distress. They often protest.

The politics of India is also changing. The Congress party is weaker. The BJP is stronger. Regional parties are important. They have power in many states. The media is vibrant and free. Social media is very active. People express their views loudly. There is a lot of debate and argument. Democracy is alive.

The future of India is uncertain but hopeful. The young people are the strength of the nation. They are creative and hardworking. They want to make India a great country. They want to see India as a leader in the world. The journey from independence till now has been long. There have been ups and downs. There have been victories and failures. But India has survived and thrived.

The people of India have a deep love for their country. They celebrate festivals of all religions. They speak many languages. They wear different clothes. They eat different foods. Yet they are one nation. This unity in diversity is the greatest gift of India.

The story of India after independence is not just about leaders and wars. It is also about ordinary people. It is about the farmer who works in the field. It is about the woman who cooks food for her family. It is about the child who goes to school for the first time. It is about the worker who builds a road. It is about the soldier who guards the border. Every person has a story. Every story matters.

The book of India is still being written. Each day adds a new page. Each year adds a new chapter. The ink is the blood and sweat of the people. The pen is their hope and determination. India will keep moving forward. It will keep striving for peace and prosperity. It will keep the light of democracy burning.

That is the story of India post independence. It is a story of courage, struggle, and triumph. It is a story of millions of dreams. It is a story that will never end.  


The story of India after independence is also the story of its states and regions. Each state has its own language, culture, and history. When India became free, the states were formed on the basis of language. This was done to make administration easier and to respect local feelings. The first state to be formed on linguistic basis was Andhra Pradesh. It was created for the Telugu speaking people. Then many other states were formed. Bombay was divided into Gujarat and Maharashtra. Punjab was divided into Haryana and Punjab. The North East got many new states like Nagaland, Meghalaya, and Mizoram. The map of India kept changing. Today there are twenty eight states and eight union territories. Each state has its own government. Each state has its own chief minister. The central government works with the states to run the country.

The Indian government has three branches. The legislature makes the laws. The executive implements the laws. The judiciary interprets the laws. The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body. It has two houses. The Lok Sabha is the lower house. The Rajya Sabha is the upper house. The members of Lok Sabha are directly elected by the people. The members of Rajya Sabha are elected by state legislatures. The Prime Minister is the head of the executive. The President is the head of the state. The President is elected by an electoral college. The President is mostly a ceremonial head. The real power lies with the Prime Minister and the cabinet. The Supreme Court is the highest court in India. It protects the constitution and the rights of the people.

The judiciary has played a very important role in India. It has given many landmark judgments. It has protected the fundamental rights of citizens. It has also checked the power of the government. In the nineteen seventies, the Supreme Court said that the basic structure of the constitution cannot be changed. This was a historic judgment. It meant that no government could destroy the democratic framework. The courts have also given judgments on environmental issues. They have ordered factories to reduce pollution. They have protected forests and wildlife. They have also given judgments on social issues. They have decriminalised homosexuality. They have allowed women to enter temples. They have given the right to privacy. The judges are independent. They do not take orders from the government. This is a sign of a mature democracy.

The media in India is very diverse. There are newspapers in many languages. There are television channels in Hindi, English, Tamil, Bengali, and many other languages. The media reports on politics, sports, business, and entertainment. It also investigates corruption and wrongdoing. The media is free but it has its own biases. Some channels are close to political parties. Some are owned by big businesses. The rise of social media has changed everything. People get news from WhatsApp, Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube. This has made information faster but also less reliable. Fake news spreads quickly. People believe what they see on their phones. The government has tried to regulate social media. It has passed laws to control content. But the debate continues.

The Indian economy has gone through many phases. In the early years, India followed the socialist model. The government controlled many industries. It set up public sector companies. It built steel plants, oil refineries, and heavy machinery factories. It also invested in education and health. The idea was to build a self reliant economy. But the growth was slow. It was called the Hindu rate of growth. It was about three to four percent per year. This was not enough for a poor country. In the nineteen eighties, some reforms were introduced. But the big change came in nineteen ninety one.

The reforms of nineteen ninety one opened the economy. Tariffs were reduced. Licences were abolished. Private companies were allowed in many sectors. Foreign companies came to India. They set up factories and offices. The information technology sector grew very fast. Indian software engineers became famous all over the world. They worked for American and European companies. The call centre industry also grew. Many young people got jobs in these industries. The service sector became the largest part of the economy. Agriculture and manufacturing also grew but at a slower pace.

The reform period also saw the rise of Indian multinationals. Companies like Tata, Reliance, and Infosys became global names. They bought companies abroad. They set up operations in many countries. Indian businessmen became billionaires. The stock market boomed. The middle class became richer. They bought cars, houses, and gadgets. They went on foreign holidays. They sent their children to private schools and colleges. The consumption of goods increased. Malls and shopping centres came up in every city. The lifestyle of people changed.

But not everyone benefited from the reforms. The farmers were left behind. They did not get good prices for their crops. They often took loans and could not repay. Many farmers committed suicide. The rural areas did not get enough investment. The schools and hospitals were still poor. The gap between the cities and the villages grew. The gap between the rich and the poor grew. The top ten percent of people owned most of the wealth. The bottom fifty percent had very little. This inequality is a big challenge.

The government has tried to help the poor. It has given subsidies on food, fertiliser, and fuel. It has built houses for the homeless. It has given pensions to the elderly. It has started health insurance schemes. It has also given free cooking gas to poor families. These schemes are expensive. They put pressure on the budget. But they are necessary for social welfare. The government also collects taxes from the rich and the companies. This money is used for public services. The GST has made tax collection more efficient. But there is still a lot of tax evasion. Many people do not pay taxes. The government is trying to bring everyone into the tax net.

The banking system in India has also changed. Earlier, all banks were nationalised. The government owned them. They were not very efficient. They had many bad loans. In recent years, the government has allowed private banks. They are more competitive and efficient. But even private banks have problems. Many of them have also given bad loans. The Reserve Bank of India is the central bank. It regulates all banks. It controls the money supply and interest rates. It also issues currency notes. The rupee is the currency of India. Its value goes up and down against the dollar. The exchange rate affects trade and inflation.

The trade policy of India has also changed. In the early years, India protected its industries. It put high taxes on imports. This was called import substitution. The idea was to make Indian goods competitive. But it did not work well. Indian goods were not of good quality. They were also expensive. In the reform era, India opened its borders. It signed trade agreements with many countries. It became a member of the World Trade Organisation. Indian goods started to go abroad. The exports grew. The imports also grew. India buys oil, gold, and electronics from other countries. It sells software, textiles, and pharmaceuticals. The trade deficit is a concern. India imports more than it exports. This puts pressure on the rupee.

The agricultural sector is still very important. Most people live in villages. They depend on farming for their livelihood. India is one of the largest producers of wheat, rice, and sugarcane. It is also a large producer of fruits and vegetables. But the productivity is low. The farms are small. The farmers use old methods. They do not have access to modern technology. The government has tried to help. It gives subsidised seeds, fertilisers, and electricity. It also buys crops at minimum support prices. But the farmers are still not happy. They protest for higher prices and more support. In recent years, the government passed new farm laws. The farmers opposed them. They camped outside Delhi for many months. The government eventually withdrew the laws. It was a big victory for the farmers.

The industrial sector is also growing. India makes cars, machines, and chemicals. It also makes consumer goods like soap, toothpaste, and clothes. The manufacturing sector employs many people. But it is not as large as it should be. The government wants to make India a manufacturing hub. It has started the Production Linked Incentive scheme. This gives subsidies to companies that make goods in India. The aim is to compete with China. Many global companies are moving their factories to India. This is good news for jobs.

The education sector has seen big changes. The literacy rate has gone up. It was only eighteen percent at the time of independence. It is now about seventy five percent. More children go to school. The government has made education free and compulsory for children up to fourteen years. It has built many schools. It has also started mid day meals. This gives children one hot meal every day. It helps them to study. The number of colleges and universities has also increased. There are now thousands of institutions. But the quality is not always good. Many graduates cannot find jobs. They do not have the skills needed by employers. The government has started skill development programs. It is also trying to reform the curriculum. The New Education Policy was introduced in two thousand and twenty. It aims to make education more holistic and flexible.

The healthcare sector is also improving. The life expectancy has increased. It was about thirty five years at independence. It is now about seventy years. Infant mortality has come down. More mothers survive childbirth. The government has started many health programs. It has built hospitals and primary health centres. It has also started vaccination drives. Polio has been eliminated. Smallpox has been eradicated. The government also gives free medicines to the poor. But the healthcare system is still weak. There are not enough doctors and nurses. The hospitals in rural areas are poorly equipped. Many people have to travel to cities for treatment. The private healthcare is expensive. Only the rich can afford it. The government has started Ayushman Bharat. This is a health insurance scheme for the poor. It covers many diseases and treatments. It is one of the largest schemes in the world.

The role of women in India has changed a lot. In the early days, women were confined to the home. They did not have many rights. They were married early. They had many children. They did not go to school. But now, more women are educated. They are working in offices, factories, and farms. They are becoming doctors, engineers, and lawyers. They are also becoming politicians. There are women chief ministers and women ministers. The Prime Minister of India has also been a woman. The President of India has also been a woman. The women are now more aware of their rights. They fight against domestic violence. They speak up against harassment. The MeToo movement also came to India. Many women shared their stories. Some powerful men were exposed. But the change is still slow. The women are not safe in public places. Many cases of rape and assault are reported. The courts have given strict punishments. But the mindset of people takes time to change.

The youth of India is the future of the country. They are more than half the population. They are energetic and optimistic. They want to start new businesses. They want to travel abroad. They want to use technology. They are connected to the internet. They use smartphones and apps. They are also politically active. They participate in protests and debates. They are not afraid to question authority. They want transparency and accountability. They are also concerned about the environment. They want cleaner air and water. They want India to be a sustainable country. The government is also trying to engage the youth. It has started many schemes for startups and innovation. It has also started internship programs. The youth have the power to shape India in the coming decades.

The defence and security of India are very important. India has a large army. It also has a strong navy and air force. The soldiers are brave and well trained. They protect the borders from enemies. India has borders with Pakistan, China, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, and Myanmar. Some borders are disputed. The border with China is called the Line of Actual Control. There have been clashes in the past. In nineteen sixty two, India fought a war with China. India lost that war. It was a big humiliation. Since then, India has strengthened its forces. In recent years, there have been skirmishes in Galwan and other areas. The Indian army has responded firmly. The border with Pakistan is also tense. The Line of Control divides Kashmir. There are frequent ceasefire violations. The Indian army retaliates strongly. The navy and air force are also modernised. India is buying new fighter planes, submarines, and missiles. It is also developing its own weapons. The defence budget is large. It is necessary for national security.

The internal security is also a challenge. There are many insurgent groups. They operate in Kashmir, North East, and some other areas. They want to secede from India. The government uses force to suppress them. It also talks to them and offers development. Some groups have come to peace. Others continue to fight. The Naxalite movement is another problem. They are left wing extremists. They operate in central India. They fight against the government and the police. They attack police stations and government buildings. The government has launched operations against them. It has also started development programs in affected areas. The people in these areas are poor and marginalised. The government tries to win their trust.

The foreign policy of India has evolved over time. In the early years, India followed non alignment. It did not join any bloc during the Cold War. It was friendly with both the United States and the Soviet Union. India also led the Non Aligned Movement. It spoke for the developing countries. It opposed colonialism and racism. It supported the freedom movements in Africa and Asia. This gave India moral leadership. But it did not get much economic help. After the Cold War ended, India changed its policy. It became closer to the United States. It also improved relations with China and Russia. It joined the BRICS group. It also joined the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation. India also has strong ties with the Gulf countries. Many Indians work there. They send money home. The diaspora is very large. It is spread across the world. They are successful in many countries. They also help India with investment and technology.

India has also played a role in global institutions. It is a member of the United Nations. It has served on the Security Council many times. India wants a permanent seat on the Security Council. It has support from many countries. But the process is slow. India also contributes to UN peacekeeping missions. Its soldiers serve in many war torn countries. India also helps other countries with development aid. It gives loans and grants to African and Asian nations. It also shares its expertise in areas like agriculture and IT. India is seen as a responsible power. It speaks for the Global South. It wants a more just and fair world order.

The cultural life of India is very rich. It has a long history of art, music, and dance. Classical music is of two types. Hindustani music is from the North. Carnatic music is from the South. Both are very old. They have many ragas and talas. The instruments are also many. The sitar, tabla, veena, and mridangam are famous. Indian classical dance includes Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi, and Kathakali. Each has its own style and story. Folk music and dance are also popular. They vary from region to region. The cinema of India is very famous. Bollywood makes films in Hindi. It is based in Mumbai. It produces many films every year. They have songs, dances, and drama. They are watched all over the world. Other film industries include Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, and Malayalam. They are also very popular. Indian actors and directors are global celebrities.

The festivals of India are colourful and joyful. Diwali is the festival of lights. People light lamps and burst crackers. They share sweets and gifts. Holi is the festival of colours. People throw coloured powder and water. They dance and sing. Eid is celebrated by Muslims. They pray and eat special food. Christmas is celebrated by Christians. They decorate trees and give gifts. Every community has its own festivals. They bring people together. They create a sense of belonging. They are a celebration of life.
