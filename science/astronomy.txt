The universe began with the Big Bang. The Big Bang happened about 13.8 billion years ago. It was not an explosion in space. It was an expansion of space itself. All matter was packed into a single point. This point was called a singularity.

The singularity was infinitely hot and dense. Then, it began to expand rapidly. This expansion was incredibly fast. It is called cosmic inflation. Inflation happened in a fraction of a second. The universe cooled as it expanded.

Energy turned into matter. The first particles were quarks and electrons. Quarks combined to form protons and neutrons. Protons and neutrons formed the first atoms. The first element was hydrogen. Helium was the second element.

A little bit of lithium was also made. These were the lightest elements. The early universe was very hot and bright. Light could not travel freely at first. The universe was like a dense fog. About 380,000 years later, things changed.

The universe became transparent. Light could finally travel freely. This leftover light is called the CMB. CMB stands for Cosmic Microwave Background. The CMB is a faint glow in space. It fills the entire universe. Scientists can detect the CMB with special instruments.

The CMB is evidence for the Big Bang. It shows the universe had a hot beginning. The CMB is almost uniform in all directions. There are tiny fluctuations in the CMB. These fluctuations are very important. They show areas of slightly different density.

Denser areas had more matter. Gravity pulled more matter into these areas. These dense regions grew over time. They became the seeds of galaxies. Galaxies are huge collections of stars. They are the building blocks of the universe.
