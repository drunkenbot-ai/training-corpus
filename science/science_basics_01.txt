The Sun gives Earth light.
The Sun also gives heat.
Plants use sunlight to make food.
This process is called photosynthesis.

A plant has roots.
Roots take water from soil.
A stem holds the plant up.
Leaves catch sunlight.
Flowers can make seeds.

Animals need food.
Food gives energy.
Energy helps animals move and grow.
A young animal is called offspring.

The human body has bones.
Bones support the body.
Muscles pull on bones.
This helps people move.

The heart pumps blood.
Blood carries oxygen.
The lungs take oxygen from air.
People breathe in and out.

Water can be solid, liquid, or gas.
Ice is solid water.
Liquid water flows.
Water vapor is a gas.

Heat can melt ice.
Cold can freeze water.
Water boils when it gets very hot.
Steam rises from boiling water.

Air is made of gases.
Oxygen is one gas in air.
People need oxygen to live.
Fire also needs oxygen to burn.

Sound is made by vibration.
A drum vibrates when hit.
The vibration moves through air.
The ear detects sound.

Light travels very fast.
A shadow forms when something blocks light.
A mirror reflects light.
A lens can bend light.

Electricity can move through a wire.
A battery stores chemical energy.
A lamp changes electrical energy into light.
A motor changes electrical energy into motion.

A force is a push or a pull.
Gravity is a force.
Gravity pulls objects toward Earth.
A ball falls because gravity pulls it down.

The Moon moves around Earth.
Earth moves around the Sun.
One year is one trip around the Sun.
One day is one spin of Earth.

Weather can change each day.
Rain falls from clouds.
Wind is moving air.
Temperature tells how hot or cold air is.

An ecosystem includes living and nonliving things.
Plants, animals, water, soil, and air interact.
Bees visit flowers.
Flowers can make seeds after pollination.

A simple experiment asks a question.
It tests one idea.
Good experiments compare results.
Careful notes help others understand.
